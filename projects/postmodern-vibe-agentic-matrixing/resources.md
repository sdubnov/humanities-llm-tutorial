# References and Resources

This project can be taught with minimal outside reading, but the following resources help explain the theoretical and methodological background.

## Dataset / corpus seed

- Collider, "Best Postmodern Books of All Time". This list is used only as an accessible seed corpus. Students should treat it as a curated classroom dataset, not as a scholarly canon or authoritative ranking.
  - https://collider.com/best-postmodern-books-all-time-ranked/

## Useful background on postmodern literature

- MasterClass, "Postmodern Literature Guide". A short general reference for students who need a quick introduction to postmodern literary traits.
  - https://www.masterclass.com/articles/postmodern-literature-guide

## Theory anchors for the agent roles

- Brian McHale, *Postmodernist Fiction*. Useful for the ontological agent because McHale famously distinguishes postmodern fiction by its foregrounding of ontological questions: worlds, boundaries, reality levels, and fictionality.

- Fredric Jameson, *Postmodernism, or, The Cultural Logic of Late Capitalism*. Useful for the affective/cultural agent because it gives students a vocabulary for pastiche, depthlessness, late-capitalist cultural saturation, and historical disorientation.

- Mark Z. Danielewski, *House of Leaves*; David Foster Wallace, *Infinite Jest*; Thomas Pynchon, *Gravity's Rainbow*; Don DeLillo, *Libra*; Kurt Vonnegut, *Slaughterhouse-Five*. These are sample literary cases, not required full readings for the short version of the lab.

## Methodological references inside the course

Students should connect this project to the course lessons on:

- structured prompting;
- interpretive taxonomies and ontologies;
- multi-turn revision;
- role-based agents;
- reproducible research artifacts;
- critical evaluation of LLM outputs.

## Instructor note on sources

For a short classroom activity, summaries are acceptable as inputs because the goal is to teach workflow design. For a research paper or advanced project, summaries are not enough. Students should replace the summary field with direct passages, instructor-provided excerpts, or a retrieval-based corpus.
