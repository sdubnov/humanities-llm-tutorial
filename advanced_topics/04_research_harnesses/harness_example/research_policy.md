# Research Policy

This harness supports metaphor annotation in short humanities texts.

## Purpose

The purpose is to produce structured metaphor annotations that are:

- grounded in exact textual evidence
- clear about source and target domains
- careful about uncertainty
- free of unsupported historical or cultural claims
- suitable for comparison across texts

## The Model May Use

The model may use:

- the source passage
- the metaphor taxonomy
- the uncertainty policy
- retrieved theory notes if provided
- prior annotations if included in the context packet

## The Model May Not

The model may not:

- invent historical context
- make cultural claims without evidence
- treat ambiguous metaphors as certain
- cite sources not provided in the packet
- ignore uncertainty notes
- assume authorial intent without support

## Human Review Required

Human review is required for:

- cultural interpretation
- historical claims
- theological claims
- final publication prose
- disputed or ambiguous annotations
- confidence scores above 4

## Stop / Revise Policy

The output should be revised if:

- required fields are missing
- exact evidence is absent
- source and target domains are confused
- confidence is unsupported
- interpretation goes beyond the provided context
- ambiguity is erased
