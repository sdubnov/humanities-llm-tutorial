# Liberty Theory Notes

A useful distinction in political thought separates negative liberty and positive liberty.

Negative liberty concerns freedom from external constraint, interference, or coercion.

Positive liberty concerns the capacity, agency, or conditions needed to act meaningfully.

A humanities analysis of liberty should ask:

- What constraint is being removed?
- What capacity is being developed?
- Who defines freedom?
- Does the text treat freedom as an event, a condition, or a process?
- Does the text preserve ambiguity between external liberation and internal transformation?
